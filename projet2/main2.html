Alexander the Great

Who is Alexander the Great

  • Alexander III emperor of Macedon between 336 BC and323 BC
  • He succeeded the throne at the age of twenty
  • Although king of ancient Macedonia for less than 13 years, Alexander the Great changed the course of history
  • he created a vast empire that stretched from Macedonia to Egypt and from Greece to part of India
  • He was a war genius known of his innovative stratigies and tactics
  • When he conquered Egypt he established the city that still bears his name: Alexandria

Greatest battles

  • Battle of Issus
  • Battle of Tyre
  • Siege of Gaza
  • Indian Campaign

what was he like

While he could be ruthless and impulsive, Alexander was also charismatic and sensible. His troops were extremely loyal, believing in him throughout all hardships. Hugely ambitious, Alexander drew inspiration from the gods Achilles, Heracles, and Dionysus. He also displayed a deep interest in learning and encouraged the spread of Hellenistic culture

His death

While in Babylon, Alexander became ill after a prolonged banquet and drinking bout, and on June 13, 323, he died at age 33. There was much speculation about the cause of death, and the most popular theories claim that he either contracted malaria or typhoid fever or that he was poisoned. After his death the Macedonian Empire fell into bloody infighting that led to its downfall. Amidst the chaos alexander's descendants and wives were assassinated.
